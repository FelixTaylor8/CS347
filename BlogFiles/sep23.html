Still having issues figuring out flex and position stuff (as usual), so I read an article explaining the two. Here are some notes from that:

  • Static is the default positioning, and it orders things in accordance to what occurs first in the HTML file.
  • Relative positioning doesn't change anything until it's shifted up, down, left, or right.
  • Absolute positioning causes all other elements to ignore it. Top, left, right, bottom, will shift it relative to the parent, but the parent also has to be absolute. I thought the parent had to be relative. That's what this site says. Probably a mistake.
  • Fixed ignores the parent element. It stays in the same spot on the screen, regardless of scrolling.
  • Sticky moves but stops once it reaches the top or bottom of the screen.

The rest of the article as stuff I pretty much already understand, nothing to note there.
