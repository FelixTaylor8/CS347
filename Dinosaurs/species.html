Velociraptor mongoliensis

Region: Mongolia
Time period: Late Cretaceous (75 million years ago)
Discovered: 1923
Diet: Carnivore
Source: Wikipedia
AMNH

This dinosaur was determined to have feathers in 2007. It couldn't fly, but it may have used its feathers "for display, to shield nests, for temperature control, or to help it maneuver while running." It used the up-turned claws on its feet to assist in killing prey.

Fun facts:

  • Velociraptor means "speedy thief"
  • The most famous fossil is one of the Fighting Dinosaurs
  • The velociraptors in Jurassic Park were actually a bigger species called Deinonychus

Spinosaurus aegyptiacus

Region: North Africa
Time period: Late Cretaceous (99 million years ago)
Discovered: Early 20th century
Diet: Carnivore
Source: Wikipedia

This unique species has had only a few fossil discoveries, and the earliest ones were destroyed in WWII. With a head shaped like a crocodile's and a sail on its back, paleontologists have theorized that it fished in rivers and swamps. Recent discoveries indicate its tail was adept for wading or swimming, but this is an ongoing debate amongst scientists.

Fun facts:

  • Spinosaurus means "spine lizard"
  • Spinosaurus was bigger than T. rex
  • Its sail was seven feet tall and its skull was six feet long

Tyrannosaurus rex

Region: Western North America
Time period: Late Cretaceous (68 million years ago)
Discovered: 1874
Diet: Carnivore
Source: Wikipedia
CBC

This apex predator was made popular by the Jurassic Park franchise. It had small but strong arms and a highly adept sense of smell. There is much debate over whether or not T. rex had feathers, lips, and whether it hunted or scavenged for prey. It used to be commonly depicted as standing upright like a kangaroo, but it's understood now that its body would have been parallel to the ground, and it used its tail to balance, like many other bipedal dinosaurs.

Fun facts:

  • Tyrannosaurus rex means "tyrant lizard king"
  • T. rex couldn't roar, they most likely made had a low rumbling sound.
  • The most famous T-rex is named Sue.

Triceratops horridus

Region: North America
Time period: Late Cretaceous (68 million years ago)
Discovered: 1887
Diet: Herbivore
Source: Wikipedia
Livescience

Triceratops is famous for its massive head with a frill and three horns, which it probably used to fight with others of its species over mates and in "fights against its main predator, tyrannosaurs." There have been 17 named Triceratops species, but only two are official.

Fun facts:

  • Triceratops horridus means "horrid three-horned face."
  • The largest Triceratops skull found is over eight feet long.
  • The first Triceratops fossil displayed is named Hatcher.
Image source

Brontosaurus excelsus

Region: North America
Time period: Late Jurassic (156 million years ago)
Discovered: 1879
Diet: Herbivore
Source: Wikipedia
Smithsonian
Scientific American

Brontosaurus is the staple example of massive dinosaurs with long necks, known as sauropods. They could feed off of trees like giraffes, but as their necks were so long, they probably couldn't hold them up high for very long, as it would have put too much strain on their hearts. In 1903, Brontosaurus was reclassified as Apatosaurus, but as of 2015, it is its own genus again.

Fun facts:

  • Brontosaurus excelsus means "noble thunder lizard."
  • There is no known Brontosaurus skull.
  • Paleontologists used to believe that Brotosaurus lived in water.
